About the Project

Our project serves to illustrate the wide-spreading effects of economic disparity, disproprtionate income differences between classes in the same society, analyzing NYC as a hyper-condensed microcosm of this phenomenon.

Vocabulary

  • 01

    Median Income

    Our infographic will present a chloropleth district map based on Median Household Income per postal code. This number divides the postal population into two equal groups: half having an income above that amount, and half having an income below that amount. We chose this specific economic aggregate for our data because we feel that it well represents the most average individual within that district.

  • 02

    Chlorpleth

    The type of map we will be using, a thematic map which colors pre-defined areas in proportion to a statisical variable, which in this case may be Median Income

  • 03

    Z-score

    this value is a standardization score for each neighbourhood which shows by how much they deviate from the parameter, which in this case is the overall NYC statistics. This number is very important, because it can put into perspective the statistical variance of each neighborhood from NYC as a whole.

Austin - MacroPage

Austin's macrosummary includes strong signifiers in terms of ways in which different sets of data can be related. This differs from mine in the sense that I had settled on representing all data in the microsummary and simply a chloropleth of median income within the macrosummary. I like Austin's approach because the signifiers allow users to visibly and consistently see the possible interconnectedness of the various data we will analyze. This visualization will also allow us to greater communicate our goal of the socioeconomic inequity and the impacts of this inequity throughout New York. In terms of usability we can implement more quality-of-life changes into this macro summary such as a zoom button, a useful signiifer, and perhaps a scale, increasing understandability. The achievability of this simply relies on us being able to generalize generating chlorpleth for the data. Furthermore, we must make sure all the data we plan to use is processed correctly.

Austin - Micropage

In terms of achievability, the by-year feature may be impossible to implement for all data-points due to the inconsistency of data formats we downloaded from the CCC, and thus would be ill-favored from the perspective of consistency. The addition of "At a Glance" helps readers from reducing these neighbourhoods to bare statistics, giving us greater cultural context. Perhaps we could somehow generate links for more information regarding these districts. The discoverability of other microsummaries from this macrosummary page is limited due to the size of the macrosummary, perhaps the macrosummary in the upper left could be a bit larger, or bolded.
